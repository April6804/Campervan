About Us

Dog Creek Campgrounds

Dog Creek Campground sits on the shores of Nolin River Lake in the rolling landscape of southern Kentucky. The large lake and surrounding woods create a recreational oasis for outdoor enthusiasts.

The campground offers 70 campsites, 24 with electric and water hookup. Other amenities include a dump station, pit restrooms, playground, boat ramp, and watchable wildlife area. Other area attractions include Mammoth Cave National Park, Wax Marina, Nolin Lake State Park, golf courses, and riding stables.

Information

Established in 2010, Bluegrass Campervans is a club of likeminded people who enjoy Class B campervans. We share great camping spots around Kentucky and we meet up once a year in the fall. We love seeing photos of everyone's set up as every campervan is unique.

The origins of the motorhome date back to 1910, when the Pierce-Arrow motor company introduced the Touring Landau model at the Madison Square Garden auto show. The Pierce-Arrow’s entry was specialized for the camper in mind—providing cargo compartments for camping equipment and even an on-board toilet.

Campervans are typically smaller than motorhomes. A Class B motorhome is also referred to as a low profile or semi-integrated motorhome. These are our favorite. The Class B motorhomes are easy to pack and drives just like a large van. A large van with a kitchen, bed, and some with a restroom! With a raised or pop-top roof, these campervans fit everything you need in a small package. It sleeps up to 2 to 4 people and is perfect for the spontaneous adventurer.
